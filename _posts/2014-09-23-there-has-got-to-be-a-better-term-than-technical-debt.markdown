[Technical Debt](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_debt) is a common term used in IT circles, and a useful metaphor when discussing planning and tradeoffs (although too often I fear management don't understand the interest repayments side of it)

Want I want is a term that is more easily understood by non technical business users and management, something which says to them "_Your half considered last minute must have change is going to leave the system in a right bloody mess, and its going to take lots of time and effort in the future to clean it up_", but in a nice punchy phrase.

Any ideas ? Some word to do with foundations, rotting, derelict, decay, structurally unsound, negleted, condemned ?
